What is Kariyasemmal, you may ask? Kariyasemmal, pronounced as ka-ree-ya-seem-maal, is a term used in the Tamil epic Ramayanam by Kambar. In the story, a sage named Visvamitra comes to the king Dasaratha and requests Rama and his brother Lakshmana to accompany him to the forest to kill monsters that are destroying the sages' abode. Visvamitra believes that Rama's strength and courage are needed to protect the sages, and he affectionately calls Rama "Kariyasemmal," which means "a man of great accomplishments."

When Visvamitra asks Dasaratha to send Rama with him, he recites the following Tamil verse, praising Rama's character and abilities:

நின் சிறுவர் நால்வரினும்
கரிய செம்மல் ஒருவனைத் தந்திடுதி
- Kambaramayanam verse 324

This verse translates to:
Among your four sons,
give me the man of great accomplishments.

This verse shows Visvamitra's admiration for Rama's skills and character, and his belief that Rama is the best fit for the mission to protect the sages in the forest.
